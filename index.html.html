chapter 02 · first project
16 jul 2025 → dec 2025 → ongoing as Oki

the local RAG.
(my first project.)

Built a retrieval-augmented chatbot from scratch. Google Forms questionnaires to the science explainers to collect real-world visitor queries. Docling to parse the documents. all-mini-cube embeddings into Chroma DB. Dolphin 3.0 (8B) running locally. CUDA + cuDNN to make it fast. Manual JSON conversion because the open-source structurers were inaccurate.

Twelve iterations. Fine-tuning rounds. Docling versus Tesseract. JSON versus Markdown structuring. Retrieval-logic rewrites. And then a hard call: the local model wasn't relevant for production. Scrapped it. Pivoted to Google Live API with document-upload referencing.

Deployed 16 December 2025 at MaskersAdda for the Journey to Mars Hackathon. With Hari on final iterations. Aparna & Inav gave it character. V1 was an intentional trial run — no data layer, the point was to test it under real visitor load.

Now it lives inside Project Octopus as Oki — the Dialogue Brain. V2 (token-optimised, data-driven) is in stakeholder discussion.

RAG v1 → v12 dolphin 3.0 chroma db scrapped local → google live api shipped 16.12.25 → Oki dialogue brain
chapter 03 · second project
jul 2025 → 14–15 apr 2026 · paper result 29 may

cupcake.
a complete
journey.

This project was not in my domain. It started as an exploration and crystallised into a single use case — Math in Motion. I was in the entire arc: exploration → build → push → outcome.

01 · origin · the exploration

First Polyscope touch in July 2025. Python through the UR Primary Client Interface. MoveJ. Inverse kinematics — complex, painful — paused. Pivoted to the RTDE protocol, cracked blend radius, then complex 3D patterns. The exploration narrowed to a single idea: render any mathematical equation as physical motion.

02 · build · the pipeline

I built the pipeline. All three Python stages — equation evaluation, normalization + joint-space transform, transmission + monitoring. The lab lead later identified and fine-tuned what I had built into a publication-ready form. Third author on the submitted paper. Public code at github.com/iria-param/Math_in_Motion.

03 · push · exploited as far as it would go

12 equations physically tested. 8 validated — Heart, Lemniscate, Butterfly, Archimedean Spiral, Batman, Lorenz, Aizawa, Rose 5-petal. 4 documented failures + 4 predicted failures. Universal Robotics engineers said real-time motion required their newer model; we pre-uploaded URScript timed with the UI on the existing UR10. Joint brainstorming with IM Lab produced the in-house acrylic barricade. The Digital team built the UI.

04 · outcome · two deliverables + a literacy

Two outcomes from one project: a live exhibit at ParSEC (14–15 April 2026) and a submitted research paper (result 29 May). And the third — the one I value most — understanding how the research team, content team, build team, and IM Lab each function.

curriculum.

Sent the first cold collaboration email of my career (BPS Space). No response. Filed it.

Then a quiet pivot. The technical problem was real, but the more valuable artefact wasn't a single bot — it was a learning path. I built a complete level- and project-wise curriculum around custom Python development and computer-vision sketch generation, and handed it to the Makerspace.

Sketch Bot lives on as a learning artefact every Makerspace user can pick up. The project becoming curriculum is the outcome.

72 people from across India. Students, professionals, engineers who took leave just to come build something. Iron Man cave energy — stripped wires, messy desks, the smell of solder, people who hadn't slept not because they had to stay up but because they couldn't stop.

chapter 06
mar — may 2026 · project octopus · vision AI

the architect
moment.

Project Octopus arrived as a lead-driven moonshot. I did not propose it. The full technical execution on my assigned problem statements lies on my hands. P1 (cross-touchpoint visitor ID + DB) and P2 (chatbot logging at 2 exhibits) — mine.

Within Octopus's Vision AI, I proposed the 3-tangent split — Room AI, Visitor Analytics, Desk Tracking — from a single base. Not handed down. My architecture call.

My current architectural approach (in progress): a symbiotic framework — vision AI built to integrate into the existing CCTV / network / access infrastructure rather than forcing the infrastructure to change for it. If it lands, the stack becomes portable to any Param location or partner site without per-site infrastructure projects.

Camera positioning was not optimal for AI use as installed. When I enquired about it, I learned the facility had already planned for additional cameras. The constraint resolved by surfacing what was already in motion — a deployment timeline to work toward.

The primitive zone tracking with basic metrics was contributed by Yatheesh. I refined that baseline and now lead the technical research phase from there.

→ technical research lead. project-level outcomes remain with team lead. 46% baseline. single-camera → multi-camera next.
